Representation

We need term limits to bring back democratic representation.

Professional politicians like to talk about the value of experience in government. Nuts! The only experience you gain in politics is how to be political.
- President Ronald Reagan

Under a republican form of government, representatives should be accountable to all people, not institutional forces like lobbyists, special interests, and partisan gamesmanship. Politicians are often unable to do their job because they are incentivized to do what it takes to get re-elected, not to do what is right for the American people.

This doesn’t make them bad people. But it does make for bad representation.
SIGN THE PETITION TO SUPPORT TERM LIMITS

Today, we have a perpetual election cycle that incentivizes politicians to speak along carefully crafted campaign talking points, constantly ask special interests for campaign donations, and rely on their political party campaign machines for election support.

And we wonder why we have such extreme partisanship in Washington?

It is because the party machines that back Hillary and Donald have extraordinary power over our representatives, our election laws, and our future. On Sunday, the Chairman of the RNC implied that the Republican Party would "punish" long-time respected leaders who don't "fall in line" and support Donald Trump.
So how can we be a nation of "we the people" if two increasingly unpopular political parties, more interested in fighting each other than representing the people, are dictating the future of this country?
Contribute $15 to Fight Back

This is why we adopted the 22nd Amendment in 1947, to limit the number of terms a President can hold office to two terms. We did this because we recognized that a President should focus on representing the people instead of playing politics.

Can a Republican support gay marriage? Not if his or her first priority is to get re-elected.

Can a Democrat vote for a tax cut? Not if his or her first priority is to get re-elected.

That's why, as President, I will be a strong advocate for term limits. I will start a conversation about how we can make elections more competitive. And I will do everything I can to make sure the American people don't have to settle for a "lesser of two evils."
 
Will you contribute $5, $15, $150, or whatever you can to start the conversation?

Run for office, spend a few years doing the job at hand, and then return to private life. That’s what Gary Johnson and Bill Weld did as governors, and that’s what all our representatives should do too.

Limit terms. Increase accountability. Bring back representation.

You in?

Gov. Gary Johnson
